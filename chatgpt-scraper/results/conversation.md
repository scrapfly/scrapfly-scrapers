## Brief history of Paris

Paris has a long and influential history stretching back over 2,000 years:

**Ancient origins (3rd century BCE – 5th century CE)**  
The area was first settled by a Celtic tribe called the Parisii along the Seine River. The Romans later conquered the region and established a city called *Lutetia*, which became an important administrative and trading center in Roman Gaul.

**Medieval period (5th – 15th centuries)**  
After the fall of the Roman Empire, Paris grew under the Frankish kings. By the 12th century, it had become a major European center of learning, home to what would become the University of Paris

. Gothic landmarks like Notre-Dame Cathedral were built during this era.

**Renaissance to Enlightenment (16th – 18th centuries)**  
Paris flourished culturally and intellectually. It became a hub for art, science, and philosophy, playing a key role in the ideas that shaped modern Europe.

**French Revolution (1789)**  
Paris was the heart of the French Revolution

**19th century modernization**  
Under Napoleon Bonaparte

**20th century to present**  
Paris endured both World Wars but emerged as a global center for culture, fashion, art, and diplomacy. Today, it is one of the world’s most visited cities and a symbol of French identity.
